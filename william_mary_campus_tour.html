The Wren Building
Oldest Academic Building ("Ancient Campus" Heart)
Built: 1695-1700
The crown jewel of the "Ancient Campus," the Wren Building is the oldest academic building in continuous use in the United States. Named after architect Christopher Wren, this Georgian brick masterpiece housed the entire college for over a century and educated presidents Jefferson, Monroe, and Tyler. The first two floors are open for tours, showcasing the historic Great Hall, Chapel, and faculty offices that have been in continuous use since colonial times.

The President's House
Historic Presidential Residence ("Ancient Campus")
Built: 1733
Part of the historic "Ancient Campus" core, the President's House has served as the official residence of William & Mary's president since 1733, making it the oldest continuously occupied president's house in the United States. This Georgian mansion has hosted countless distinguished guests and continues to serve its original purpose today, completing the triangular layout of the Ancient Campus alongside the Wren Building and Brafferton.

Brafferton Building
Historic Indian School ("Ancient Campus")
Built: 1723
Part of the historic "Ancient Campus," this building originally housed the Brafferton Indian School, funded by the will of scientist Robert Boyle. One of the oldest buildings in the United States, it represents an early attempt at cross-cultural education. Today it houses administrative offices and stands as a reminder of the complex history of colonial-era Native American relations.

Crim Dell Bridge
Campus Romance & Tradition
Built: 1960s
This charming wooden footbridge over Crim Dell pond is steeped in William & Mary tradition and folklore. According to campus legend, couples who kiss on the bridge are destined to marry, while those who break up must jump off the bridge to break the spell. The bridge has become one of the most photographed and beloved spots on campus.

üö∂‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è Recommended Campus Walking Tour
Ancient Campus & Historic Core Route: Begin at the iconic Wren Building (first two floors open for tours) - the heart of the historic "Ancient Campus." Walk around the Sunken Garden, the ceremonial center surrounded by the Ancient Campus buildings. Visit the President's House and Brafferton Building to complete the "Ancient Campus" triangle dating from the early 1700s. Stroll to the romantic Crim Dell Bridge for photos and campus tradition. Continue to Blow Memorial Hall for administration, then explore Jefferson Hall and Ewell Hall for academic heritage. Head to Swem Library and the Integrated Science Center for modern facilities. Visit the Marshall-Wythe Law School and Miller Hall, with optional stops at the Muscarelle Museum, Phi Beta Kappa Hall, Kaplan Arena, and Williamsburg Baptist Church. The full tour covers approximately 2.5 miles and showcases over 330 years of American higher education history, from the colonial "Ancient Campus" to cutting-edge modern facilities.
