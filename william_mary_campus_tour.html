The Wren Building
Oldest Academic Building in Continuous Use
Built: 1695-1700
The crown jewel of William & Mary, the Wren Building is the oldest academic building in continuous use in the United States. Named after architect Christopher Wren, this Georgian brick masterpiece housed the entire college for over a century and educated presidents Jefferson, Monroe, and Tyler.

Historic Core Route: Begin at the iconic Wren Building and explore the historic heart of campus. Visit the President's House, then walk to see the Brafferton Building. Continue to Jefferson Hall and Ewell Hall to experience the academic tradition. Head to Swem Library for the modern academic center, then explore the Integrated Science Center for cutting-edge research facilities.
